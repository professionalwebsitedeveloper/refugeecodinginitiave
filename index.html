About Us

The Refugee Coders Initiative is a non-profit organization dedicated to providing technology education and digital skills training to refugees in Kakuma camp.

We believe in the transformative power of technology to create opportunities and improve lives.

Founded in 2022, our initiative has already trained over 200 refugees in various digital skills, helping them to build careers and become self-sufficient.

Our Mission

Our mission is to bridge the digital divide by providing accessible, high-quality technology education to refugees in Kakuma. We aim to:

  • Equip refugees with marketable digital skills
  • Provide access to technology and learning resources
  • Create pathways to employment and entrepreneurship
  • Foster a community of tech-savvy individuals
  • Promote digital inclusion and empowerment

Courses Requirements

Our programs are open to all refugees in Kakuma camp. Here's what you need to join:

  • Basic literacy in English (all instruction is in English)
  • Commitment to complete the full program (3-6 months depending on course)
  • Willingness to learn and work in a team environment
  • No prior tech experience required for beginner courses
  • For advanced courses: completion of beginner level or equivalent knowledge
  • Access to a smartphone (we provide computers for in-class work)
  • Completed application form (available at our center)

We provide all necessary learning materials and equipment during training sessions.
